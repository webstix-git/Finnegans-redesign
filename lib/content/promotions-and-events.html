Annual traditions

The nights people plan
their calendars around

The best traditions aren't fancy. They're the ones you look forward to every year.

Every winter
Finnsmas · Christmas at Finnegan's

Some bars hang
a wreath

We cover the place in lights. Every winter, Finnegan's transforms into Finnsmas — ceilings disappear behind wrapped presents, thousands of lights line the walls, and seasonal drinks hit the menu. Ugly sweaters become acceptable attire. Actually, encouraged.

People who haven't seen each other all year squeeze around tables, raise a glass, and pretend they don't know every word to Mariah Carey. For many regulars, it's part of their holiday tradition.

A little crowded. A little loud.
A whole lot of fun.

St. Patrick's Season

Our favorite
time of year

Being an Irish pub means March hits a little differently around here. The green decorations come out, the Guinness flows, and green beer finds its way into plenty of hands. Traditional Irish fare returns to the menu.

From pub crawls to St. Patrick's Day itself, downtown Springfield comes alive — and Finnegan's is right in the middle of it. Whether you're Irish by blood or just Irish for the day, you're welcome here.

Sláinte.
